Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 15, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how VILOSTUDIOS ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information when you use our website, apply for positions, or interact with our services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

We collect information that you voluntarily provide to us, including:

  • Application Information: Name, email address, phone number, portfolio links, CV/resume, cover letters, genre specializations, and any additional information you provide during the application process
  • Contact Information: Information provided when you contact us through our website, email, or other communication channels
  • Account Information: If you create an account or log in, we collect your login credentials and profile information

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information, including:

  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent on pages, click patterns, and navigation paths
  • Device Information: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, and screen resolution
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience and analyze website traffic

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Application Processing: To review, evaluate, and process job applications and assess candidate qualifications
  • Communication: To communicate with you about applications, opportunities, updates, and respond to your inquiries
  • Service Improvement: To analyze website usage, improve our services, and enhance user experience
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes
  • Security: To protect our website, services, and users from fraud, abuse, and security threats
  • Business Operations: To manage our business operations, including talent database management and recruitment processes

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • Internal Use: With authorized VILOSTUDIOS personnel involved in recruitment and hiring decisions
  • Service Providers: With third-party service providers who assist us in operating our website and conducting business operations (e.g., hosting, analytics, email services)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government regulation, or to protect our rights and safety
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, where your information may be transferred as part of the transaction

4. Data Protection and Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Secure server infrastructure and access controls
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Limited access to personal information on a need-to-know basis

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. Application data may be retained for recruitment purposes even after a position has been filled, unless you request deletion.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, including:

  • Access: Request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal and operational requirements
  • Objection: Object to certain processing activities
  • Data Portability: Request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the "Contact Us" section below.

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to collect and store information about your preferences and activities on our website. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. However, disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

Types of cookies we use:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the website to function properly
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings

8. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, services, or applications. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child, we will take steps to delete such information promptly.

10. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from those in your country. By using our services, you consent to the transfer of your information to these countries.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the updated policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us:

  • Email: Through our official communication channels
  • Website: Visit our website for contact information
  • Address: VILOSTUDIOS, Dubai, United Arab Emirates

We will respond to your inquiries in a timely manner and in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

Vilostudios – Freelancer Terms of Service (TOS)

Last Updated: January 15, 2025

These Terms of Service ("Terms") govern the relationship between Vilostudios ("Vilostudios," "the Studio," "we," "us") and any independent contractor, freelancer, or contributor ("Freelancer," "you") who applies to, collaborates with, or performs services for the Studio.

Vilostudios operates as a Multi-Purpose Studio (MPS) providing services across, but not limited to:
Animation (Hybrid, 2D, 3D), Music, Sound Design, Voice Acting, Storyboarding, and Writing.

By applying to Vilostudios, accepting assignments, or contributing work in any capacity, you acknowledge and agree to these Terms.

1. Independent Contractor Status

All freelancers engaged by Vilostudios act strictly as independent contractors. Nothing in these Terms creates an employment relationship, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

Freelancers are solely responsible for their own taxes, insurance, equipment, and legal obligations unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2. Mandatory Portfolio Contribution (Non-NDA)

As a condition of application and continued collaboration with Vilostudios:

  • All freelancers must contribute at least one public, non-NDA portfolio piece created in association with the Studio.

This contribution is mandatory and serves as:

  • Skill verification
  • Portfolio development
  • Studio showcase material

These works are publicly accessible and not protected under NDA.

Failure to comply may result in rejection of application or termination of collaboration.

3. Mandatory Annual Demo Reels (Animators & Sound Designers)

3.1 Requirement

Animators and Sound Designers are required to participate in two (2) mandatory demo reels annually, held one to two times per year, subject to studio scheduling.

Each demo reel includes:

  • Duration: 1–3 minutes total
  • Cuts: Each talent may contribute 1 to 5 cuts maximum
  • Compensation: Unpaid
  • Nature: Participation is mandatory

These demo reels are studio initiatives and a requirement for continued collaboration.

3.2 Usage Rights

By participating, freelancers grant Vilostudios and all involved collaborators the right to:

  • Publish, distribute, promote, and host demo reels
  • Use the works for marketing, studio showcases, and portfolio references
  • Allow other credited participants to share and reference the work

These demo reels are public works and may be used by all credited contributors without time limitation.

4. Distribution & Platforms

Vilostudios reserves the right to upload and distribute portfolio works and demo reels on platforms including, but not limited to:

  • YouTube
  • X (formerly Twitter)
  • Other platforms that do not utilize machine-learning systems to scrape, train, or exploit creative works without consent

Vilostudios actively avoids platforms that engage in unethical creative data extraction.

5. Educational & Skill Development

During demo reel and portfolio productions:

  • Freelancers may share techniques and workflows
  • Vilostudios may provide supervision, mentorship, and feedback
  • The intent is continued skill refinement and professional growth

These initiatives are considered studio-guided development programs, not speculative or exploitative labor.

6. Creative Direction & Leadership

Portfolio works and demo reels may be directed or supervised by:

  • Episodic Directors
  • Animation Directors promoted internally

Creative direction may be collaborative but remains subject to final approval by Vilostudios.

7. Client Projects & NDA Obligations

7.1 Client Project Confidentiality

All projects undertaken for clients through Vilostudios are strictly confidential and governed by Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs).

Freelancers may not, without prior written authorization:

  • Disclose client identities or project details
  • Share assets, scripts, audio, visuals, or work-in-progress materials
  • Publicly reference client involvement
  • Use client work for portfolios unless explicitly approved in writing

Confidentiality obligations apply before, during, and after project completion or termination.

8. Studio Operations & Proprietary Information

Freelancers acknowledge that Vilostudios utilizes proprietary software, tools, pipelines, workflows, and production methods, including internal creative and operational systems.

All such information is confidential.

Freelancers may not discuss, disclose, teach, publish, replicate, or demonstrate any proprietary Vilostudios operations outside the studio without prior written confirmation from authorized senior leadership.

9. Public Exceptions

The only exceptions to confidentiality obligations are:

  • Portfolio pieces explicitly designated as non-NDA
  • Mandatory demo reels
  • Content released through official Vilostudios channels

No work is considered public without explicit confirmation.

10. Professional Conduct & Good Faith

10.1 Mandatory Professional Behavior

Freelancers must act in good faith and maintain a professional production environment at all times.

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

  • Disrespectful or unprofessional communication
  • Toxic behavior, harassment, intimidation, or hostility
  • Personal disputes or interpersonal conflicts interfering with production
  • Any conduct that delays, disrupts, or destabilizes workflow

Vilostudios may issue warnings or remove freelancers from productions if behavior negatively impacts the team or schedule. Severe or repeated violations may result in immediate termination.

10.2 Deadlines & Performance Standards

Freelancers are required to respect all assigned deadlines.

Failure to deliver work on time three (3) or more times within a single calendar month may result in:

  • Removal from the current production
  • Suspension from active assignments

If missed deadlines become a regular occurrence, Vilostudios reserves the right to:

  • Terminate collaboration entirely
  • Remove the freelancer from the Vilostudios talent database
  • Decline future project assignments

11. Scheduling, Availability & Communication

Freelancers are responsible for managing their own schedules and availability.

All talents must:

  • Maintain an accurate personal work calendar
  • Communicate availability, constraints, and changes in a timely manner
  • Report availability directly to Production Assistants, Directors, and authorized studio leadership

Freelancers are expected to:

  • Confirm availability before accepting assignments
  • Notify the production team immediately of any conflicts
  • Respond within reasonable timeframes to scheduling or availability requests

Failure to properly communicate availability resulting in missed deadlines, production delays, or scheduling conflicts may be treated as a performance issue.

12. Freelancer Database & Work Availability Disclaimer

Inclusion in the Vilostudios freelancer database does not guarantee immediate work, recurring assignments, or consistent project availability.

Vilostudios operates on a commission-based and project-greenlight model. Periods of inactivity may occur.

Vilostudios is not responsible for guaranteeing workload, income, or continuous engagement.

13. Exclusivity

Freelancer Database Personnel

Inclusion in the Vilostudios freelancer database does not create an exclusivity obligation. Freelancers remain free to work for other studios, clients, or projects outside Vilostudios.

Director Positions

Freelancers who are promoted to a Director-level position (via performance, promotion, or other studio appointments) are typically considered studio-exclusive.

This exclusivity includes a commitment to prioritize Vilostudios projects over external work.

Director-level positions typically receive:

  • Higher and regular payment
  • Potential full-time engagement
  • Expanded responsibilities and authority within the studio

Transition to Exclusivity

Promotion to a Director position will be accompanied by formal confirmation in writing, outlining payment, duties, and exclusivity terms. Freelancers not promoted remain non-exclusive and may work elsewhere.

14. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

These Terms shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Emirate of Dubai and the federal laws of the United Arab Emirates.

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to these Terms, the freelancer's engagement, or services provided to Vilostudios shall be finally resolved by arbitration in Singapore under the rules of the Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC).

The arbitration shall:

  • Be conducted in English
  • Take place in Singapore
  • Be binding and final, with the award enforceable in any competent jurisdiction

Freelancers agree to waive any rights to sue in courts except for enforcement of the arbitration award.

15. Enforcement & Authority

All decisions regarding conduct, performance, scheduling, warnings, removals, or termination are made at the sole discretion of Vilostudios and its authorized leadership.

16. Acceptance of Terms

By applying to Vilostudios, submitting work, or participating in any studio initiative, you confirm that you:

  • Have read and understood these Terms
  • Accept the mandatory portfolio and demo reel requirements
  • Agree to confidentiality, conduct, scheduling, performance, exclusivity, and availability standards
  • Acknowledge the public and non-NDA nature of designated works
